# Coreimpact.ai Website — Build Brief

## Purpose
Single-page marketing site. One job: get qualified prospects onto a free 30-minute call. Everything on the page serves that goal or gets cut.

## Design System

### Sacred — Do Not Deviate
- **Background:** #f3f2eb (warm cream/off-white)
- **Typography:** Bold sans-serif set in true black (#000000). Condensed serifs for accent/editorial texture.
- **Composition:** 80s editorial design. Text-first layouts inspired by the golden era of print advertising (Apple, Nike, Volkswagen campaigns of the era). Bold headlines, generous whitespace, confident typography.
- **Texture:** Subtle print-era artifacts. Think Aldus PageMaker output, hand-cut layouts, slight grain. Not glossy or digital-clean. The imperfection is the character.
- **Tone:** The decade that embraced modern technology with enthusiasm. We're doing the same thing, just with AI instead of the Macintosh.
- **Reference artist:** April Greiman — early Mac-era graphic design that pushed limited tools to maximum creative potential.

### Color Palette (from live site)
- Primary background: #f3f2eb (warm cream)
- Primary text: #000000 (true black)
- Accent: Orange/amber (the orange from the current site — used on headline keywords, CTAs, badges)
- Dark sections: Deep brown/charcoal (#1a1a1a or similar) with cream text — used for hero, alternating content blocks
- Cards on dark bg: Slightly lighter dark (#2a2a2a) with subtle rounded corners
- Cards on light bg: White or near-white with subtle shadow

### Typography (from live site)
- Headlines: Bold condensed serif (Playfair Display or similar high-contrast serif). This is the signature 80s editorial look. Large, dramatic, italic for accent words.
- Body: Clean sans-serif (likely Inter or similar), generous line height
- Labels/categories: All caps, small, sans-serif with letter-spacing (80s print convention)
- Accent words in headlines are set in orange italic serif

### The 3D Showcase Placeholder
- Static hero image or subtle animation placeholder for the Phase 2 interactive 3D space station visualization
- **Aesthetic: 80s sci-fi.** Think Tron (1982), Alien ship interfaces, WarGames terminal displays. Wireframe geometry, CRT phosphor glow, scanlines, vector graphics on dark backgrounds.
- This is the one dark element on the page — it should feel like looking through a window into a command center
- Text overlay: "See your AI system. Live." or similar teaser
- Must harmonize with the cream/editorial surround — the contrast is intentional (analog editorial wrapping a digital core)

### 6. AI Ops Audit (Smart Form)
**Section header:** "Not Ready for a Call? Start Here."

A self-service form that collects enough info for us to do a quick written assessment. Lower friction than booking a call. Submissions route to Merlin for analysis → Pete reviews draft response → sends.

**Form fields:**
1. Your name
2. Company name
3. Industry (dropdown: Construction/Trades, Hospitality/F&B, Healthcare, Agriculture, Retail/E-commerce, Real Estate, Legal, Logistics/Transport, SaaS/Tech, Financial Services, Professional Services, Manufacturing, Education, Other)
4. Team size (dropdown: Just me, 2-10, 11-50, 51-200, 200+)
5. "What software or tools does your business rely on day-to-day?" (free text, placeholder: "e.g. Xero, Salesforce, Google Workspace, custom CRM...")
6. "What takes the most time in your team's week that feels like it should be automated?" (free text)
7. "Have you tried any AI tools? What happened?" (free text, placeholder: "e.g. ChatGPT, Zapier AI, nothing yet...")
8. "If you could wave a magic wand and automate one thing in your business tomorrow, what would it be?" (free text)
9. Email address
10. "Anything else we should know?" (optional free text)

**Submit button text:** "Get Your Free Assessment"
**Post-submit message:** "Thanks. A real human (backed by some very capable AI) will review your answers and reply within 48 hours."

**Backend:** Form submissions POST to a webhook or email. For MVP, email to pete@coreimpact.ai (or a shared inbox). Phase 2: webhook to Merlin for automated triage.

### 8. FAQ
Adapted from Kiwi.ai, rewritten for global audience:

1. **"Do I need to know anything about AI before the call?"** Not a thing. If you know your business, that's all we need. We handle the AI part.

2. **"What if AI isn't right for my business?"** We'll tell you. Honestly. We'd rather save your money than sell you something that doesn't fit.

3. **"What's the difference between the free call and the Discovery Session?"** The free 30-minute call is exploratory — you'll get a sense of whether AI is relevant and some initial direction. The $99 Discovery Session is a structured 90-minute deep-dive with a written report: full opportunity map, top quick wins, and a prioritized action list delivered within 48 hours.

4. **"What industries do you work with?"** All of them. Construction, hospitality, healthcare, legal, logistics, SaaS, financial services, retail, real estate — if you run a business, we can find AI opportunities in it.

5. **"Who actually builds the AI systems?"** We do. In-house. Every system we deploy is built by our engineering team, not outsourced. That's why we can guarantee they work.

6. **"Is the free call a sales pitch?"** No. It's a conversation. If AI isn't the right move for you, we'll say so and you'll have lost nothing but 30 minutes. You'll leave with more clarity than you started with regardless.

7. **"What happens after the call?"** Entirely up to you. If AI looks promising, you might want a Discovery Session for the full deep-dive. If not, no follow-up spam, no pressure.

## Content Pipeline (Post-Launch)

### AI Ops Audit Form Flow
1. Prospect fills out the form on coreimpact.ai
2. Submission arrives (email or webhook)
3. Merlin analyzes the submission: industry context, tool stack assessment, quick-win identification
4. Merlin drafts a personalized response (2-3 paragraphs + specific recommendations)
5. Pete reviews and approves/edits the draft
6. Response sent to prospect's email
7. Target: under 24 hours from submission to response
